You can choose one of the following topics as your exam project:

(1) Under typical weather conditions, air temperature decreases with height above the ground. In a temperature inversion, temperature increases with height. This is a common phenomenon associated with the inflow of warm air over a cooled ground. Propose a model for temperature inversion and determine the dependence of air density on temperature. Why does thermal inversion affect the persistence of smog? The temperature should be modified in the Python code – it will no longer be constant. Remain within the ideal gas approximation. It is acceptable to limit the application to 1D.

(2) Assume air (particles of mass m1) polluted with small dust particles, each mass m2 (m2 ≥ m1), resulting in a system of particles A and B. The Python program's original code assumes a single ideal gas component in 1D; for this project, you will need to extend it to handle two distinct ideal gas species, ensuring only that particles A and B exclude each other's positions in the simulation.  So, particle A can be displaced into the location of another particle A, but it is not possible if it is particle B. No particle displacement takes place then.  Determine the appropriate barometric formula for polluted air, limiting to 1D as before.

(3) The temperature increase DT under constant solar energy is given by DT=Q/(M*c), where DT is the temperature increase, Q is the supplied solar energy, M is the gas mass, and c is the specific heat. The Python code assumes only a fixed value for temperature T. To address the increase in gas temperature, you should modify the code to support different specific heat values. Illustrate how the graph of the barometric formula changes for several values of specific heat when the same amount of solar energy is supplied.
